Murder Mystery Developer

Along with board games, I also enjoy designing social experiences for large groups of friends. Unlike traditional murder mysteries (in which viewers eat dinner, watch live actors act out scenes, and discuss among themselves who they think are the killers), my take on this genre of world-building turn the viewers into participants. Here, everyone is assigned certain in-game powers and various objectives (packaged as a "role"), and must engage in a sort of court-like politicking and social cooperation/sabotage in order to achieve their goals. All my murder mysteries are zero-sum, so players have a direct impact on other players, which in turn drives the overall momentum of the game.

For example, in a past pirate mystery, the crew's captain was found dead in his chamber and the rest of the 13 crewmates (aka the players) must now decide what to do. Some players cared about solving the murder, others cared about promoting to be the captain, and "other" others wanted to trade away their wares. The effect of this is that every single character has a limited but unique perspective on the entire story with interweaving gives-and-takes. The joy in the game is piloting your role in the way that best matches your personal playstyle, while experiencing this 13-faceted mega-narrative unfold... live! I've hosted 5 of these murder mysteries in both in-person and online formats. I'm looking forward to hosting more!

Tabletop Roleplaying Game: Dungeon Master ("DMing")

I also run custom tabletop roleplaying campaigns built-in from my own Dungeon-and-Dragons-inspired homebrew system. Combining my board game and murder mystery experiences, these campaigns are similar to traditional games except with a heavy visual-aid component to it (lots of corresponding pieces game tokens).

For those unfamiliar, the Dungeon Master (DM) is responsible for designing the game world, narrating the story, and enforcing the rules. Acting as a storyteller and referee, the DM creates quests, describes environments, and controls non-player characters. They manage the logistics of the game, improvise when needed, and ensure an engaging and balanced experience for the players!

In a nutshell, the DM is like the author that is live-writing a book's story before you, except you're able to interact with the story and change its outcomes as it unfolds before you!
