I am a junior AI Safety researcher currently staffed to Dr. Koen Holtman (Dutch representative on JTC21, committee for technical standards drafting for the EU AI Act and European Union). I have 3 years of experience in both technical and governance research. I am committed to developing a robust, international policy that properly safeguards our AI-intertwined future from malicious actors.

My governance research interests lie in GPAI risk management systems in both soft and hard law contexts (eg. auditing, guidelines, regulation). My technical research interests lie in GPAI vulnerabilities, particularly in data extractability vs. data privacy. The GPAI that I am primarily focused on are generative AIs like LLMs or foundation models.

I completed a bachelor's degree in analytical philosophy from the University of Pennsylvania, as well as, formal coursework in political science and mathematics. Additionally, I have 2 years of experience in the private sector. My multidisciplinary education and professional experiences enable me to effectively incorporate industry best practices into regulatory processes.

I am especially eager for AI policy & strategy positions in the US federal space and governance research positions at AI labs.
